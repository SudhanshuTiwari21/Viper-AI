## Your workspace

The folder you open in Viper is your **workspace**. Features that need context—chat, search, indexing—use files **inside that folder** (and paths the app is allowed to read). Don’t open a directory that contains secrets you never want in context unless you exclude or relocate them.

## What may leave your machine

To generate answers, **prompts and relevant excerpts** are sent to the model provider your organization configures. Treat chat like any cloud AI tool: avoid pasting passwords, production keys, or regulated data you’re not allowed to share.

## Sensitive projects

- Prefer a **dedicated clone** or branch for experiments if policy requires it.
- Keep credentials in **environment files or secret managers**, not in files you reference casually in chat.
- If your team has **data residency** requirements, confirm the deployment matches policy before using hosted models.

## Local vs hosted

The Viper app runs on your computer; **the AI service runs in the cloud** so you get answers without running anything yourself. If your employer uses a **private** Viper deployment, your IT team can explain where requests go.
