---
title: Troubleshooting
description: Fix common problems when using the hosted Viper app—chat not loading, replies cutting off, or things feeling stuck.
---

These steps assume you’re using **Viper as a normal app** (download or web) that talks to **our hosted service**. You should not need to run servers or developer tools.

---

## Chat won’t load or says it can’t connect

1. **Check your internet** — Open another site or app to confirm you’re online.
2. **Sign out and sign back in** — Session or cookie issues sometimes show up as a generic connection error.
3. **VPN or work network** — Some offices block real-time features. Try another network (e.g. phone hotspot) or ask IT whether traffic to Viper’s domains needs to be allowed.
4. **Update the app** — Install the latest version from the place you got Viper; older builds can misbehave after we ship fixes.

---

## Replies stop halfway or never start

- **Wait a few seconds** — Long answers can pause briefly; scrolling up sometimes shows text still arriving.
- **Send a shorter follow-up** — Huge pasted logs or files can hit limits; try summarizing in your own words or attaching less at once.
- **Start a new thread** — If one conversation is stuck, a fresh chat often recovers.
- **Quit and reopen Viper** — That clears a bad local state without touching your account.

---

## Search or “smart” context feels empty or wrong

- **Make sure the right folder is open** — Viper uses the workspace you opened; switch workspace if you’re in the wrong project.
- **Try again after a short wait** — Right after opening a large repo, indexing can still be catching up.
- If it **never** improves, **contact support** with what you expected to find—we can check whether something’s wrong on our side for your account.

---

## Still stuck?

Use **Support** on this site or the in-app help channel your team uses. Include what you were doing, any error text you see, and whether it happens on one network or everywhere—that helps us reproduce it quickly.

If you **build or host Viper yourself** (engineers only), use [Build from source](/docs/guide/for-developers) in the docs sidebar instead of this page.
